Core Findings

1. Growth Without Jobs

Employment elasticity collapsed to 0.01 (2011-2016), meaning GDP growth generated virtually no jobs. The recovery to 1.11 (2017-2023) was driven by precarious gig work, not formal employment.

2. Colonial-Era Inequality

The top 1% income share reached 22.6% in 2022‚Äîhigher than during British rule. This represents the most extreme concentration of wealth in independent India's history.

3. Undermined Federalism

Through cesses, GST design, and conditional transfers, the Centre reduced states' fiscal autonomy despite constitutional protections, creating de facto unitarism.

4. Statistical Manipulation

The Statistical Suppression Index rose from 2.3 to 7.8, documenting systematic interference in data production, including suppressed surveys and indefinite census postponement.

5. Democratic Backsliding

Multiple international indices document erosion: Freedom House downgrade, V-Dem's "electoral autocracy" classification, and press freedom ranking falling to 161/180.

Event-based coding of government interference in statistics: 

SSI_t = Œ£(severity_i √ó salience_i) / n

- n: number of events in year t
