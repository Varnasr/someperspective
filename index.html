The Core Finding

This study reveals a fundamental transformation in India's political economy between the UPA (2004-2014) and NDA (2014-2025) periods. While both regimes maintained strong GDP growth, the character and distribution of that growth diverged dramatically.

Under UPA, growth averaged 7.7% and created formal employment while maintaining federal fiscal balance. Under NDA, growth averaged 6.8% but concentrated benefits among the top 1%, while employment became increasingly informal and states lost fiscal autonomy through systematic centralization.

Finding 1: The Great Jobs Vanishing Act

Simply put: Good jobs with security and benefits have largely disappeared. In 2004, 18% had formal jobs. Now it's just 11%. The new "jobs" are delivery, contract work, daily wages‚Äîno security, no benefits, no future. Young graduates face 23% unemployment.

Research Methodology

The Data Suppression Challenge

Since 2017, the Indian government has systematically delayed, revised, or completely suppressed key economic datasets. This created an unprecedented methodological challenge: how do you study an economy when the government controls what data you can see?

Our Solution: Data Triangulation

Official Sources (When Available)

  • ‚Ä¢ National Sample Survey Office (NSSO)
  • ‚Ä¢ Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS)
  • ‚Ä¢ Reserve Bank of India reports
  • ‚Ä¢ Ministry of Finance budgets
  • ‚Ä¢ Election Commission data

Independent Sources

  • ‚Ä¢ CMIE Consumer Pyramids Survey
  • ‚Ä¢ World Inequality Database
  • ‚Ä¢ V-Dem Democracy Index
  • ‚Ä¢ Reporters Without Borders
  • ‚Ä¢ Freedom House ratings

Novel Methodological Contributions

1. Statistical Suppression Index (SSI)

First quantitative measure of government interference in statistical production. Codes events by severity and salience: SSI = Œ£(severity √ó salience)/n

2. Employment Quality Decomposition

Separates formal/informal employment using PLFS and CMIE data, controlling for definitional changes in 2017.

3. Fiscal Centralization Measurement

Tracks effective (not nominal) devolution by including cesses, surcharges, and conditional transfers. Reveals how federalism was undermined through accounting.

Validation and Robustness

Cross-validation

Key findings verified across multiple independent datasets. CMIE-PLFS unemployment correlation: r=0.89

Temporal consistency

Trends robust to different base years and seasonal adjustments. Results hold for 2011-12, 2004-05 constant prices.

International benchmarking

India's trajectory compared with Turkey, Brazil, Hungary using harmonized measures. Patterns confirmed.

Limitations and Caveats

  • ‚Ä¢ Some data gaps remain unfillable (e.g., 2017-18 consumption survey)
  • ‚Ä¢ Indices aggregate complex phenomena into single numbers
  • ‚Ä¢ Causal identification limited by political economy constraints
  • ‚Ä¢ Interstate variation masked by national averages
